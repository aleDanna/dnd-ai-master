# Cultures, Factions, Settlements


### 4.1 Settlement Archetypes

Pick a clear archetype when introducing a new place:

- **Hamlet (~50 pop)** — one tavern (maybe), one shrine, one elder. Simple needs, simple troubles.
- **Village (~500 pop)** — a market square, a few specialized crafters, a constable, a priest.
- **Town (~5,000 pop)** — multiple districts, a council, a temple, several inns, one or two factions.
- **City (~25,000 pop)** — districts with personalities (Docks, Temple, Merchant, Slum, Noble), competing factions, organized crime, watch.
- **Metropolis (100k+)** — city + sprawl + multiple ruling powers + every faction has a chapter. Use sparingly; cities of this scale need real prep.

### 4.2 Common Faction Archetypes

Every settled area has factions. Use these as building blocks:

- **The Watch / Guard** — keep order; honest or corrupt depending on town.
- **The Merchant Guild / Council** — money interests, often the de facto rulers.
- **The Temple / Church** — moral authority, sometimes political.
- **The Thieves' Guild** — organized crime, smuggling, fences.
- **The Adventurers' Guild** — issues bounties, fields parties, mediates with rulers.
- **The Mages' Guild / Academy** — arcane learning, often suspicious.
- **The Ranger's Lodge** — wilderness defense, druidic ties.
- **The Cult** — clandestine group with a hidden agenda; often the antagonist.

A small town might have 2-3; a city has all of them, layered, with internal rivalries. Never give a settlement a faction "just because" — every faction must want something from the player or be in the player's way.

### 4.3 NPC Quick Generators

When you need an NPC on the fly, pick:
- **Profession** (innkeeper, blacksmith, priest, sailor, farmer, scribe, fence, courier, scholar)
- **Bond** (loves their family / owes a debt / serves a cause / hides a secret)
- **Mannerism** (always polishing something / squints / never makes eye contact / laughs at wrong moments)

Three picks = a memorable NPC in five seconds.

### 4.4 Languages

Every D&D world has standard languages:

- **Common** — universal trade tongue
- **Racial languages** — Dwarvish, Elvish, Halfling, Gnomish, Orcish, Goblin, Giant, Draconic
- **Exotic** — Abyssal (demons), Infernal (devils), Celestial, Sylvan (fey), Undercommon (Underdark)
- **Druidic** — secret druid cant
- **Thieves' Cant** — secret rogue cant

When NPCs speak, mention the language if it matters: a written contract in Infernal, a ward in Celestial, scratchings in Druidic. Players paying attention to languages should be rewarded with extra information.

### 4.5 Trade and Currencies

D&D economies run on a copper/silver/gold standard:
- **1 cp** ≈ a meal of bread and water; **1 sp** ≈ a hot stew; **1 gp** ≈ a night at a respectable inn or a day's skilled wages.
- **5 gp** ≈ a longsword; **50 gp** ≈ chain mail; **1500 gp** ≈ plate armor.
- **Platinum (pp)** = 10 gp each, used by the wealthy.
- **Electrum (ep)** = 5 sp each, an antique alloy in some settings.

Ground player wealth in real-world equivalents: 75 gp is a respectable adventurer's purse, not a small fortune. Magic items priced in thousands of gp signal real wealth.
