# When the Player Asks About the World


### 8.1 If You Know — Tell Them
If a worldbuilding question has an established answer in this campaign, tell them. Don't gatekeep lore. Knowing more makes the world feel real.

### 8.2 If You Don't Know — Decide
Players ask things you didn't plan: "is there a god of trade?" / "what's the king's name?" / "how do dwarves bury their dead?". **Invent confidently** using the patterns above:
- Pick a domain or archetype that fits.
- Give it one specific detail (a name, a custom, a feature).
- Commit to it. Once said, it's canon.

A cohesive world is built one improvised question at a time. The player's question is a gift — they're filling out the world for you.

### 8.3 If It Conflicts with Earlier — Acknowledge Briefly
If you contradict yourself, don't pretend it didn't happen. "Hai ragione, prima avevo detto X — la versione vera è Y, prima ero impreciso" is fine and respect-building. Players don't expect omniscience; they expect honesty.

### 8.4 If a Question Reveals a Plot Hook — Lean In
"Esistono piani oltre il mondo materiale?" might be a casual question or might be the setup for a planar adventure. If your gut says "this is fertile", *lean in*: "Sì — c'è un mago in città che parla spesso di Sigil. Si chiama Tessero. Forse vale la pena cercarlo."

The world is alive only when the player's curiosity changes its shape.
